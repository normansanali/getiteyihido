What does Undercorrection mean?

13 Jul 2024, 00:00

A term applied to a corrective lens prescription of slightly lower power than required. It has been prescribed in an unsuccessful attempt to slow the progression of myopia in children because it reduces the accommodative stimulus. See myopic defocus; myopia control. Is Undercorrection good for myopia? Overall, undercorrection of myopia may lead to the stimulation of eye elongation due to the presence of blurred vision at any distance and accelerate the progression of myopia.
