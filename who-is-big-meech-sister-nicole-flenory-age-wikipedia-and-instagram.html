    Who Is Big Meech Sister Nicole Flenory?

    Nicole Flenory is the well-known sister of Big Meech. Her brothers are Demetrius aka Big Meech and Terry aka Southwest T.

    We cannot find much about Nicole herself. But she belongs to the infamous mafia family along with her brothers.

    The new series will mainly focus on the family battles with rival gangs. It is basically about the journey of two brothers from Detroit to the Americas’ biggest drug empire.

    Detroit-based siblings start out in the dangerous business while they were in high school.

    The role of Demetrius is played by his younger son Demetrius Junior. Big Meech is the one who builds up the empire along with Terry until the late 80s.

    In 2002, Flenory started a record label named BMF Entertainment. It was a promoter to many famous hip-hop stars and was associated with Young Jeezy, Trina, and Fabolous.

    Finally, in 2005 the lead gang members were finally arrested for their crimes. During the year BMF Entertainment transporting hundreds of Kgs of Cocaine to many drug hubs such as Atlanta, Detroit, etc.

    Know about Nicole Flenory age and wikipedia

    Nicole Flenory has reached the age of 46 years. She is not yet featured on Wikipedia.

    Nicole is the youngest member of the Flenory family. She was born in 1974 in Dartmouth St., Detroit.

    Charles Flenory and Lucille Flenory are parents to the younger Flenory. She grew up with her two siblings.

    The father of Nicole was a guitarist for House of God and Keith Dominion. He was the one to establish Gospel Sounds Record Corporation in 1963.

    Charles wrote a song named Jump for Joy in 1997 for Campbell Brothers. The song was featured on the album Pass me not and became on of the hit single.

    Nicole seemed to have lived a normal life despite her close association with drug dealers. She currently spends her peaceful life in Lincoln Park, Michigan.

    Laila Pruitt plays the role of Nicole in the series BMF. She is known for her role as Nikita in the movie Everything’s Gonna be Okay.
