    Yes, you heard it right. Kisaki is no more. Takemichi, our determined hero, took on the challenge of protecting Hina without resorting to murder. He aimed to neutralize Kisaki, but fate had other plans. Kisaki met his end in a shocking vehicle accident. Imagine Takemichi’s relief – he successfully thwarted the threat that Kisaki posed to Hina’s life. This turn of events not only brought closure to the conflict but also shook the very foundation of the narrative, reminding us of the unpredictability of fate.

    What Happens After Kisaki Dies? The Kanto Incident Aftermath

    After Kisaki’s demise, the story takes a somber turn. The Kanto Incident concludes with the arrest of the remaining S-62 members, marking the end of a tumultuous chapter. The casualties, including Kisaki, Emma Sano, and Izana Kurokawa, paint a poignant picture of the human toll of the conflict.

    As the survivors grapple with the weight of the tragedy, Takemichi visits the wounded Kakucho in the hospital. Here, we witness the loss of Tenjiku’s dreams following Izana and Kisaki’s deaths. Despite the sorrow, Takemichi encourages Kakucho to shape Tenjiku’s destiny, emphasizing the enduring trauma left by Kisaki’s demise. The narrative delves into the complex aftermath, exploring the emotional and psychological toll on the characters as they navigate a world reshaped by the consequences of the Kanto Incident and Kisaki’s death.

    Is Kisaki a Time Leaper?

    Now, let’s clear up a bit of confusion. Is Kisaki a time leaper in Tokyo Revengers? No, he’s not. In a pivotal arc, Takemichi directly questions Kisaki about manipulating time, and the answer is a resounding no. However, the plot thickens as we learn that Kisaki is familiar with the concept of time leaping.
    This revelation unveils a mysterious connection between characters with time-manipulating abilities, adding layers of complexity to the narrative. While Kisaki isn’t a time leaper himself, his knowledge of this extraordinary power contributes to the unfolding mysteries within the Tokyo Revengers universe, leaving us intrigued about what other surprises await us.

    Who is Kisaki in Tokyo Revengers? 

    Let’s rewind a bit. Who is this Kisaki character, anyway? Kisaki Tetta is the main antagonist in Tokyo Revengers. He’s a cunning and manipulative gangster with a singular goal – to seize leadership of the Tokyo Manji Gang and make it the largest criminal organization in Japan. He becomes a personal adversary to our protagonists Takemichi Hanagaki, Mikey, and Draken, causing pivotal tragedies, including the deaths of characters like Hinata Tachibana and Keisuke Baji.
    Despite his villainous nature, there’s a surprising twist. Following a final time leap, Kisaki undergoes a transformation and is invited by Takemichi to join the founding members of the Tokyo Manji Gang, altering the course of their destinies. In the present timeline, Kisaki becomes a top administrator of Toman, orchestrating intricate schemes and conflicts within the gang. His character is marked by complex motivations, romantic obsessions, and a tragic end resulting from a confrontation with Takemichi.

    Tokyo Revengers Plot: A Brief Recap

    For those who might need a quick refresher, Tokyo Revengers follows the gripping narrative of Takemichi Hanagaki. This 26-year-old discovers the tragic deaths of his ex-girlfriend Hinata and her brother Naoto at the hands of the Tokyo Manji Gang. Through an unexpected time-travel ability, Takemichi embarks on a mission to alter their fate.
    The plot unfolds through various arcs, including clashes with rival gangs, intricate alliances, and betrayals. It explores critical moments in gang conflicts, such as the Moebius, Valhalla, and Bloody Halloween arcs, revealing the intricate dynamics within the Tokyo Manji Gang. As the story progresses, it introduces new adversaries like Kisaki and rival gangs like Tenjiku, leading to intense confrontations and the exploration of themes like friendship, redemption, and the profound impact of one’s choices.
