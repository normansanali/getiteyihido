    Remembering River Phoenix Twenty Years After His Tragic Death River’s first taste of massive success came with the 1986 Rob Reiner coming-of-age classic Stand By Me. He plays a troubled teen who sets out on an adventure, along with three friends — played by Corey Feldman (pictured), Wil Wheaton and Jerry O’Connell — to find a dead body. In this melodrama, River plays a kid uprooted to the jungles of a Central American nation when his offbeat inventor father decides to build an ice factory in the middle of the nowhere. The parents are played by Harrison Ford and Helen Mirren. Opposite Sidney Poitier, River co-stars as a boy whose parents are suspected of being Soviet sleeper agents. River was channeling Kurt Cobain before channeling Kurt Cobain was a thing. River was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor as the gifted son of two activists running from the law for their part in a crime committed long ago during an anti-war protest. River was genuinely thrilled when Kevin Kline edged him out at the awards ceremony. They would co-star in I Love You to Death the following year. River co-starred with Martha Plimpton in both Running on Empty and The Mosquito Coast. They also dated in real life. River attended the Academy Awards ceremony with Plimpton, who would also co-star in Ron Howard’s Parenthood that year with Leaf Phoenix. He played a young Indiana Jones in the threequel’s opening sequence. The dark comedy co-starred Kevin Kline and Keanu Reeves. Riding with Keanu in the Gus Van Sant indie. The last River Phoenix movie released before his death, The Thing Called Love was about aspiring country musicians. It co-starred a pre-Speed Sandra Bullock. Fans built this memorial outside the Viper Room shortly after his death on October 31, 1993. R.I.P., River. You are missed. River Phoenix: A Life in Pictures
