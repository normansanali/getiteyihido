    Who is Ronan Farrow?

    American Ronan Farrow is a Journalist, lawyer, activist, and contributor writer. He is renowned as the host of Ronan Farrow Daily.

    Last, in 2020, he appeared in the TV series, Good Morning America.

    Ronan Farrow- Birth, Age, Parents, Ethnicity, Siblings

    Ronan Farrow was born on the 19th of December 1987 in New York City, New York. His birth name is Satchel Ronan O’Sullivan Farrow.

    His father, filmmaker, Woody Allen is of Jewish ethnicity whereas his mother, actress, Mia Farrow is of Catholic ethnicity.

    He has two adopted siblings Moses and Dylan. Besides them, she has 13 half-siblings from his father’s and mother’s sides.

    Education

    Ronan Farrow attended the Center for Talented Youth and took courses skipping grade school. Later, he joined Bard College at Simon’s Rock. However, he took a transfer from the college to join Bard College.

    From the College, he earned a bachelor’s degree with Honors in Philosophy. During graduation, he was only 15 years old which made him the youngest person to graduate from the institute.

    Later, in 2009, he received a J.D from Yale Law School. After that, he joined Magdalen College, Oxford, and studied Philosophy. At the time, he researched the subject, of Exploration of Poor, and Developing Countries.

    Ronan Farrow- Professional Life, Career

    Back in 2001, Ronan Farrow became a Youth Spokesman for UNICEF. As a spokesperson, he advocated for the children and women of Sudan’s Darfur region. He continued in the position till 2009.

    During his days at Yale Law School, he worked with the law firm, Davis Polk & Wardwell as an intern.

    In 2009, Ronan Farrow became a special adviser for the Obama administration. At the time, he was involved in Humanitarian and NGO Affairs. Also, he was a special representative for Afghanistan and Pakistan.

    In 2011, Secretary of State Hillary Clinton appointed him as a special advisor on Global Youth Issues. He left the position in 2012.

    After that, he started pursuing Journalism as his career. Also, he has contributed various editorial pages for newspapers like The Wall Street Journal, The Atlantic, and The Guardian. 

    Also, he started making an appearance on the TV programs like Ronan Farrow Daily, Today, and The View as a host and correspondent.
